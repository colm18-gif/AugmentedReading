AI Augmented Reading is a patented method available for licensing to publishers, platforms, distributors and device manufacturers across the digital reading ecosystem.

IP status

The patent

A patent application for AI Augmented Reading has been filed with the Intellectual Property Office of Ireland. The application protects the complete five-step method — from reader text selection through AI classification, routing, visual generation and inline display.

Patent Application
PTIE20260000000265
Short-Term Patent · Patents Act 1992, Section 63
Intellectual Property Office of Ireland · Filed April 2026
Inventor: Colm O'Connor · Waterford, Ireland
Paris Convention window open to April 2027

The patent protects the method, not the AI model or technology stack used to implement it. A licensee may use any AI model and may use different models for different visual categories within the same title. The method applies equally across EPUB, PDF and web-based reading interfaces.

What is protected

The method, not the implementation.

Any system implementing the core five-step method requires a licence, regardless of the underlying technology, platform or content type.

Reader-initiated text selection. The reader highlights any descriptive passage of five or more words.
AI classification. The selected passage is classified into one of ten predefined visual categories.
Routing of the classified passage. The system selects the appropriate output type for the recognised content category.
Visual generation. A contextually appropriate visual is generated — animated diagram, AI illustration, or contextual insight.
Inline display with position preservation. The visual appears within the reading interface. The reader's position is preserved on dismissal.
Commercial structures

Flexible licensing for any partner type.

Non-exclusive licences allow multiple partners to implement the method simultaneously, while exclusive arrangements remain available for partners who want defined-territory or defined-vertical protection.

Option A
Non-Exclusive Licence
Available to multiple partners simultaneously. The standard structure for platform operators, publishers and distributors. Licence fee plus activation-based royalty.
Option B
Exclusive Licence
Available for a defined period or territory. Prevents equivalent rights being granted to direct competitors within that scope. Higher fee reflecting the exclusivity.
Option C
Pilot Agreement
A time-limited, metrics-defined structure for partners who wish to validate the concept before committing to a full commercial licence.
Who can license

Relevant across the full digital reading ecosystem.

AI Augmented Reading applies wherever readers encounter descriptive prose in a digital format. Academic and medical publishers, digital reading platforms, ebook distributors, library services, e-reader device manufacturers and educational publishers have all been identified as natural licensing partners.

The comprehension argument is strongest where content is most technically dense — medical, scientific and professional education publishing — but the method applies across all descriptive prose in any subject area.
